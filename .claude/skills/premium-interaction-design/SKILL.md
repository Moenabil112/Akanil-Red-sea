# Premium interaction design

Interactions must make the institutional story easier to understand.

## Interaction hierarchy

Prioritize:

1. orientation;
2. comprehension;
3. progression;
4. confidence;
5. delight.

Do not reverse this order.

## Preferred patterns

- persistent but quiet navigation;
- section progress or active-section indication;
- deliberate anchor transitions;
- progressive disclosure for complex institutional content;
- interactive value-chain stages;
- route and node exploration;
- comparison between Morocco capability and Sudan opportunity;
- sticky narrative panels where the content warrants it;
- responsive hover, focus and selected states;
- concise tooltips for status and evidence.

## Rules

- Every hover interaction requires a keyboard focus equivalent.
- Every interactive item must have a clear selected, disabled, loading and error state where relevant.
- Touch targets must be comfortable on mobile.
- Do not hide essential content behind hover.
- Do not hijack scrolling.
- Do not use custom cursors unless they add a specific navigational function.
- Avoid carousel dependence for critical content.
- Avoid page transitions that delay access to information.
- Avoid 3D tilt on standard cards.

## Responsive behavior

Author a distinct mobile interaction model:

- simplify motion;
- prioritize vertical narrative;
- replace dense maps with focused node sequences when needed;
- keep language switching and institutional contact reachable;
- preserve content order and evidence labels.

## Completion check

An interaction is justified only when you can finish this sentence:

“This interaction helps the stakeholder understand ______.”

If the answer is merely “the site feels more dynamic,” simplify or remove it.
