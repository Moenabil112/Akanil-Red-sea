# Moroccan stakeholder UX

Design for a Moroccan decision-maker who evaluates institutional credibility before visual novelty.

## Stakeholder groups

- ministries, agencies and regional institutions;
- export and business organizations;
- industrial groups and manufacturers;
- banks, insurers and investors;
- logistics and infrastructure operators;
- technology, training and professional-service providers.

## First-visit questions to answer

Within the first viewport and the next two sections:

1. Who is Akanil?
2. What is the Gateway?
3. Why does it matter to Morocco?
4. What is Sudan's role beyond raw materials?
5. What is being requested from the stakeholder?
6. What is confirmed and what remains a target or proposal?

## Morocco value proposition

Give visible emphasis to:

- industrial transformation;
- standards and quality;
- finance and insurance;
- export capability;
- technology and skills;
- regional distribution;
- value-chain partnership.

Do not frame the proposition as charity, general diplomacy or an event invitation alone.

## Trust cues

Use:

- precise institutional language;
- controlled status labels;
- evidence and date markers;
- clear Akanil ownership;
- restrained calls to action;
- process visibility;
- French and Arabic editorial quality.

Avoid:

- fake counters;
- unverified logos;
- broad “partner with us” language;
- startup waitlists;
- aggressive sales copy;
- unexplained futuristic dashboards.

## Calls to action

Preferred actions are specific:

- request an institutional briefing;
- explore a priority value chain;
- discuss company qualification;
- review the Forum activation model;
- contact Akanil through a controlled channel.

Each CTA must state what happens next and must not imply automatic acceptance, investment or partnership.
