# Institutional art direction

Create a visual system that feels deliberately art-directed by a senior human designer.

## Core character

The interface should be:

- Moroccan and institutionally credible;
- calm, premium and spacious;
- contemporary without appearing fashionable for its own sake;
- cinematic in selected moments;
- grounded in economics, industry, relationships and evidence;
- culturally informed without becoming decorative folklore.

## Composition principles

1. Use strong editorial hierarchy and controlled asymmetry.
2. Alternate scale, density and rhythm across sections.
3. Preserve generous negative space around leadership statements.
4. Use cards only when the information is truly modular.
5. Prefer full-width editorial compositions, split narratives, diagrams and layered grids over repeated card rows.
6. Let typography and layout carry the design; imagery is supporting evidence or atmosphere.
7. Use decorative Moroccan, Amazigh, Nubian and Red Sea motifs sparingly as structural geometry, borders, masks or transitions.

## Color

Use approved tokens. The dominant visual field is deep Atlantic and midnight blue, with ivory for reading, Moroccan green for sustainable and institutional value, Nile blue for water and data flows, and copper or gold accents for routes and decisive actions.

Avoid:
- neon;
- purple startup gradients;
- rainbow data visualization;
- gold everywhere;
- low-contrast text on textured imagery.

## Typography

- Arabic: Alexandria or approved fallback.
- French and English: Inter or approved fallback.
- Do not imitate luxury branding through extreme letter spacing in Arabic.
- Maintain readable line lengths and clear text rhythm.
- Use display scale selectively, not in every section.

## Imagery

Classify each visual as one of:

- documentary;
- conceptual;
- atmospheric;
- diagrammatic;
- generated concept.

Generated concept images may support atmosphere and motion, but must not dominate every section or imply a real event, partner, facility or route.

## Required review

For every major page, explain:

- the visual hierarchy;
- the role of each image;
- why the composition fits the stakeholder;
- which elements communicate trust rather than decoration.

Reject a layout that could be relabelled for another consultancy, conference, crypto project or AI startup without structural change.
