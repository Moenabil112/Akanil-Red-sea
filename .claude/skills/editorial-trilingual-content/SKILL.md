# Editorial trilingual content

Maintain one institutional meaning across three independently edited languages.

## Canonical names

Use the approved names from governing sources. At minimum preserve:

- Akanil for Development and Investment.
- Akanil Morocco–Red Sea Economic Gateway.
- بوابة أكانيل الاقتصادية بين المغرب والبحر الأحمر.
- Passerelle économique Akanil Maroc–mer Rouge.
- Morocco–Sudan Economic Forum.
- المنتدى الاقتصادي المغربي السوداني.
- Forum Économique Maroc–Soudan.

Do not invent a legal translation when the corporate evidence does not confirm one.

## Content architecture

Store content separately from components, with independent AR, FR and EN records.

Each record should support:

- heading;
- supporting statement;
- body;
- CTA label;
- CTA explanation;
- status label;
- evidence or source note where applicable;
- image alt text.

## Editorial tone

Arabic:
- clear, institutional and naturally RTL;
- avoid translated corporate clichés.

French:
- suitable for Moroccan institutions and enterprises;
- precise, calm and professionally edited.

English:
- international business-development language;
- avoid startup jargon.

## Claims

Classify claims internally as:

- approved fact;
- verified current fact;
- approved strategic proposition;
- target;
- assumption;
- conceptual design statement.

Do not flatten these into identical public language.

## Interface writing

- Prefer concrete verbs.
- Explain the next step.
- Avoid inflated claims such as “revolutionary,” “world-leading” or “unmatched.”
- Avoid repeating the project name in every heading.
- Keep buttons short but not vague.

## Review

For every section, compare all three languages for:

- equivalent meaning;
- status accuracy;
- tone;
- length and layout impact;
- terminology consistency;
- culturally appropriate phrasing.
