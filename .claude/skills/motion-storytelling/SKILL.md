# Motion storytelling

Motion is a narrative layer, not a decoration layer.

## Story beats

Use motion to communicate:

- opening a gateway;
- movement between economic nodes;
- qualification and progression;
- transformation from resource to shared value;
- continuity from Forum activation to year-round follow-up;
- trust through controlled access and visible audit.

## Recommended sequences

### Hero

- slow environmental push;
- controlled logo or gateway reveal;
- one primary route or light flow;
- text appears through masks or opacity, not excessive kinetic typography.

### Corridor

- route lines draw in sequence;
- nodes activate with status-aware pulses;
- water or data flow remains subtle;
- map layers move at different depths;
- conceptual status is visibly labelled.

### Value chains

- reveal stages in order;
- carry one visual signal through sourcing, processing, finance, logistics and market;
- animate only the active chain.

### Forum

- treat the Forum as a first activation event;
- transition from meeting or delegation imagery into commitments and follow-up;
- avoid celebration-only motion.

### Trust and AI

- show consent, policy, source and human review as a controlled sequence;
- never imply autonomous approval.

## Technical rules

- Prefer CSS or platform-native animation for simple states.
- Use a mature motion library only where choreography or scroll coordination warrants it.
- Use SVG or canvas for semantic route animation.
- Use MP4 or WebM for cinematic raster motion.
- Use Lottie only for genuinely vector-based assets.
- Use GIF only for previews, not primary production media.
- Pause offscreen animation.
- Avoid permanent GPU-heavy effects.
- Provide poster and reduced-motion alternatives.

## Review question

At every animation breakpoint ask:

- What information changed?
- What relationship became clearer?
- What state did the user understand?

Remove motion that answers none of these.
