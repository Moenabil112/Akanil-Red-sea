## Trade-Chain Africa

- Category: trade-corridor structuring and stakeholder coordination platform.
- Purpose: structure trade routes, compare corridor scenarios, connect
  production regions with ports, organize aggregation/shipment requirements,
  map customs/inspection/standards needs, support documented decisions.
- Geography referenced: KAEC + King Abdullah Port, Ain Sokhna, Aswan
  economic/logistics node, Port Sudan, Bosaso, Asmara–Massawa corridor,
  Tanger Med (Moroccan manufacturing and market-access node).
- Never presented as: shipping line, freight forwarder, port operator,
  customs authority, guaranteed transport service.
- Public status: structured-project · Evidence: public-summary-available.

## Valura

- Category: economic-zone and value-addition development model.
- Locations referenced: Northern State, Kassala, Gedaref (Sudan).
- Purpose: transform production regions into connected economic nodes
  (aggregation, sorting, testing, storage, primary processing, manufacturing,
  utilities, water/energy, logistics, digital infrastructure, services,
  market linkage).
- Controlled labels only (structured development project, under institutional
  review, evidence-controlled, site validation required). Never "operational
  economic zone", "government-approved zone" or "licensed free zone".
- Public status: institutional-review · Evidence: controlled-evidence-available.

## RWAFID — روافد

- Category: agricultural crops, feed and livestock supply-chain platform.
- Scope: grains, sesame, groundnuts, oilseeds, fodder, feed concentrates,
  inputs; livestock incl. veterinary requirements, traceability, quarantine,
  feed, land/sea transport, cold chain, slaughter/processing, hides and
  by-products.
- Value creation may occur at source in Sudan, in African production/economic
  zones, at Red Sea industrial nodes, in Saudi Arabia, Egypt or Morocco.
- Never reduced to an export brokerage; presented as supply-chain structuring,
  qualification and market-access platform.
- Public status: structured-project · Evidence: controlled-evidence-available.

## IBRIZ / GAAS (ADR-016)

- IBRIZ — digital gold and banking-infrastructure project; GAAS — Gold as a
  Service for regulated financial institutions.
- Public narrative limited to intended product architecture (gold-supported
  digital account infrastructure, asset-backed value representation,
  traceability, compliance-oriented infrastructure, banking integration
  modules, GAAS capabilities for regulated institutions).
- **Never** presented as an operating or licensed bank; no account-opening
  CTA, deposit-taking, payment services, investment solicitation,
  convertibility promises, asset-backing guarantees or return figures.
- Mandatory regulatory note (localized in all three languages): IBRIZ and GAAS
  are presented as financial-technology and banking-infrastructure projects;
  any regulated activity remains subject to applicable licensing, regulatory
  approval and institutional deployment agreements.
- CTA: technology-data-partnership (technology and regulated-infrastructure
  discussion) — never a consumer banking CTA.
- Public status: regulated-development · Evidence: evidence-restricted.

## Card contract (§26)

Every platform card shows: name, category, strategic purpose, economic problem
solved, Akanil's role, main geographies, relevant audiences, public status,
evidence state, controlled CTA — and never unverified investment values,
expected returns, confidential sponsors, private counterparties, ownership
claims without evidence, logos without permission, or "fully bankable" /
"investment ready" labels.
