# P2 — Claims and Scenario Boundaries

Companion to `docs/p0/P0-CLAIMS-AND-EVIDENCE-BOUNDARIES.md` and
`docs/p1/P1-CLAIMS-AND-CONTENT-REGISTER.md`. Enforced by
`tests/p2-value-chains.test.ts`.

## Everything on `/value-chains` is a scenario

The overview states it up front and each profile repeats it: the six pathways
are potential, indicative scenarios for study, grounded in Akanil's approved
value-chain baseline (and, where noted, the VALURA blueprint, the RWAFID concept
and the Trade-Chain Africa corridor architecture). Nothing on these pages
confirms a partner, buyer, financier, route, tariff, transit time, cost or
approval.

## What is never stated or implied

- No market volume, production figure, price, tariff, transit time, margin or
  return. Scanned in the English copy (`tonnes/day`, `USD n`, `n%`, "tariff of",
  "transit time of", "guaranteed return/financing/price" are all absent).
- No confirmed supplier, buyer, financier, carrier, port, zone or authority.
  Related platforms are ecosystem roles, not confirmed operations.
- No operational route. Corridors are labelled conceptual and require current
  logistics and regulatory review.

## Regulated and sensitive elements

- **Mining and mineral value:** marked *Regulated or sensitive elements*. No
  reserve, grade, volume, price or return; no confirmed resource, buyer,
  financier or custody arrangement; identity, compliance and traceability are
  stated as prerequisites ("no anonymous flows"). A mandatory regulatory note
  makes any pathway strictly subject to verified ownership, licensing,
  compliance, traceability and current regulatory review; reception of a request
  is not acceptance, and no financing, custody or convertibility is offered.
- **IBRIZ / GAAS** appears against the mining and ports pathways only as a
  *potential regulated enabling layer, never active financing* — no active
  financing, custody or settlement for any value chain.

## Shared-value framing

Sudan is a value-chain partner, never merely a raw-material source. Each flow
stage names where value is contributed on both sides of the bridge (Sudan,
Morocco, corridor). The specialist-review pathway (reused from P1) restates that
AI assists but never approves, reception is not acceptance, and only authorized
humans decide.

## Reception boundaries (unchanged from P0/P1)

The chain-aware reception adds context only. It introduces no backend, storage,
cookies, uploads, accounts, case numbers or automatic submission; information
leaves the page only when the visitor explicitly opens, copies or downloads the
prepared request. Invalid chain ids are ignored (fail safe).

## Carried-forward missing inputs

All P0/P1 missing-input items (`docs/p0/P0-MISSING-INPUTS-REGISTER.md`) remain
unresolved and are unchanged by P2. No pathway status is upgraded and no
withheld claim is published until controlled evidence is supplied.
