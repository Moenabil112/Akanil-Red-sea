# ADR-012 — Red Sea ecosystem product positioning

Date: 2026-07-19 · Status: accepted · Supersedes the "introduction window"
framing of ADR-007 for public positioning (the clarity principles remain).

## Context

The approved P0 directive repositions the public product from a cautious
institutional introduction website to the public representation of an
operating economic and technology ecosystem spanning Morocco, Sudan, Saudi
Arabia, Egypt and the Horn of Africa.

## Decision

- Adopt the approved product definition (ecosystem operator, not window) and
  the six public roles for Akanil (founder, executive operator, orchestrator,
  structuring operator, coordinator, technology/evidence governance operator).
- Rebuild the homepage in the fixed 14-section order with "Why the Red Sea"
  and the value flow before any limitation matrix.
- Keep the binding hierarchy Akanil → Gateway → Forum → portfolio platforms
  distinct in all languages.
- Remain evidence-led: repositioning changes emphasis, not claim discipline.

## Consequences

The status layer moved from section 2 to section 13; audience paths grew from
five to seven (ADR-014); the corridor page became "The Red Sea supply-chain
architecture". Visitor-facing "corridor intelligence" wording was replaced
(economic corridor analysis / Red Sea corridor architecture); the term remains
allowed in internal documentation only.
